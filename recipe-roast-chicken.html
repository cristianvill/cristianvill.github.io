Perfect Roast Chicken

This is my desert island recipe—the one I come back to again and again. A simple roast chicken is one of those things that seems straightforward until you try to actually make a good one. This version gives you crispy, golden skin and meat that stays juicy. No fancy techniques required, just attention to a few key details.

Prep Time 15 minutes
Cook Time 1 hour 15 min
Total Time 1.5 hours
Servings 4 people

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (3.5-4 lbs), giblets removed
  • 2 tablespoons kosher salt (or 1 tbsp table salt)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or softened butter
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 1 head of garlic, halved crosswise
  • 4-5 sprigs fresh thyme (or rosemary, or whatever herbs you have)
  • 1 onion, quartered (optional)

Instructions

  1. Dry the chicken thoroughly. This is the most important step for crispy skin. Pat the chicken completely dry with paper towels, inside and out. If you have time, let it sit uncovered in the fridge for a few hours or overnight—this dries the skin even more.
  2. Season generously. Salt the chicken all over, inside and out. Don't be shy—the salt needs to season the meat through the skin. Add black pepper. If you dried the chicken in the fridge, take it out 30 minutes before roasting to bring it to room temperature.
  3. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). You want it hot.
  4. Stuff the cavity. Squeeze the lemon halves over the chicken, then stuff them into the cavity along with the garlic halves and herbs. This isn't really about flavoring the meat (that's a myth), but the aromatics will perfume your kitchen nicely.
  5. Tie the legs (optional but nice). Use kitchen twine to tie the legs together. This helps the chicken cook evenly and look prettier. If you don't have twine, don't stress about it.
  6. Oil the skin. Rub the outside of the chicken with olive oil or butter. This helps with browning.
  7. Roast. Place the chicken breast-side up in a roasting pan or cast iron skillet. If using onion quarters, scatter them in the pan—they'll pick up the drippings. Roast for about 1 hour 15 minutes, or until the thickest part of the thigh reaches 165°F on an instant-read thermometer. The juices should run clear.
  8. Rest before carving. This is crucial. Let the chicken rest for 15 minutes before cutting into it. This allows the juices to redistribute. Use this time to make a quick pan sauce if you want.

Notes & Variations

The crispy skin secret: Really, it's all about drying the bird. The salt helps too by drawing out moisture. If you want next-level crispy skin, try dry-brining overnight.

Temperature matters: Get an instant-read thermometer. It's the difference between perfectly cooked chicken and dry, overcooked meat. I pull mine at 160-165°F in the thigh.

Spatchcocking: If you want to cut the time in half and get even more crispy skin, spatchcock the chicken (remove the backbone and flatten it). Same temperature, but it'll be done in 45-50 minutes.

Pan sauce: Pour off most of the fat from the roasting pan, add a splash of white wine or chicken stock, scrape up the brown bits, and reduce. Instant sauce.
